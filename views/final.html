What is Group Health Insurance

• Healthcare is growing expensive day by day
• Any hospitalization due to simple reason or a complex surgery can burn a hole in your pocket.
• You may not always be prepared with money to pay for these expenses.
• Health Insurance is an effective way to manage such uncertainties in life and handle unforeseen health expenses.
• Group Health Insurance (GHI) is an insurance policy purchased by a company to cover their employees, with an option also to cover their family members such as spouse, children and parents.
• Unlike a regular health policy which has waiting period to claim for certain kind of ailments, GHI enables employees and
dependents to be covered for such ailments from day 1 of getting added to policy.

Coverage Specifics: Hospitalization

• Hospitalization: Admission in hospital for a minimum duration of 24 hours, except for daycare procedures.
• Daycare procedure is any treatment taken under General or Local anesthesia and does not require hospitalization for 24 hours due to advancements in medical science.
• Hospital is defined as any registered medical center providing in-patient or day-care treatment, having atleast 10 in-patient beds in a tier-2 city/town and having atleast 15 in-patient beds in a tier-1 city or metro city.
• Need for hospitalization: If the patient is admitted in hospital only for the purpose of diagnostic tests, or when the treatment could have been provided on out-patient basis, then such claims are not payable by insurance company.

• Room Rent Limit: This limit is inclusive of per day room/ward charges of hospital and nursing charges.
• Duty doctor charges, if billed separate by hospital, are not payable.
• When patient occupies a room whose rent+nursing charges is more than the limit, then proportionate deduction will take place.
• Proportionate Deduction: Assume patient’s per day room rent is Rs 7000, while limit is Rs 5000 as per policy, then the extra Rs 2000 is 28% of the actual room rent. This is the proportionate deduction, meaning, insurance will deduct 28% from the hospital bill and only pay 72% of bill because of proportionate deduction rule. A percentage of bill is proportionately deducted by insurer whenever you take a room category above room rent limit.

Limitation of Health Insurance Policy

• Pays upto sum insured only.
• Admissions where need for hospitalization is not clear or patient could be managed on OPD basis and when treatment is not covered by policy are not payable by insurance,
• Excluded expenses are payable by patient.
• Proportionate deduction & copay share (wherever applicable) are payable by patient.
• Cashless facility is not available in every hospital, patient has to go for reimbursement claim in non-network hospital.
• All OPD and diagnostic expenses (when there is no hospital admission or daycare procedure) are non payable.

Claim Procedure: Cashless Claim

1. Employee visits network hospital with insurance card. Visit > this link to check list of network hospitals.
2. Hospital requests employee to fill Pre-authorization form (Preauth). Hospital sends Preauth request to insurance company (48 hours prior to a planned hospitalization & within 24 hours in emergency case).
3. Insurer issues a Preauth approval letter when treatment is covered within policy.
4. At the time of discharge, hospital sends final bill and discharge summary to insurer for final approval.
5. Insurer sends final approval with approved amount to hospital. The non-payable charges have to be borne by employee.
6. Employee needs to claim pre and post hospitalization( within 7 days of post hospitalization bill) OPD expenses through Reimbursement claim.


Claim Procedure: Reimbursement Claim

1. Reimbursement claim with duly filled form and all supporting documents have to be submitted to insurer within 15 days from date of discharge.
2. If there are deficiencies in claim documents, insurer will raise additional document request. Claim will not be paid till all required documents are submitted.
3. Settlement will take place within 30 days from date of submission of last necessary document. Payment will be via NEFT.


FAQs

• Can I add my dependents just before hospitalization?
->You are only allowed to add dependents at the time of policy renewal or at the time of your joining.

• When is mid-term inclusion of dependents allowed?
->Mid-term inclusion is allowed in case of (a) Spouse, on account of marriage during policy term (b) Childbirth.

• What if my claim is rejected?
->It is possible the claim gets rejected if (a) treatment is not covered by policy (b) sum insured has exhausted (c) need for hospitalization is not justified. Consult with Customer Hotline to check claim applicability.

• If I want to take patient home earlier than 24 hours, will there be a problem?
->Policy will only pay for a covered treatment when there is min. 24 hr hospitalization (except when treatment is day care). Insurer has the right to reject claim when duration is less than 24 hrs.

• Is it possible to apply cashless in more than one policy?
->Cashless is allowed only in one policy. However, if sum insured gets exhausted and some amount remains unpaid by insurance, that can be claimed from any other health insurance policy. Speak to customer hotline for more information.

• Can capped procedure cost be claimed over more than 1 health policy?
->If 2 or more insurance policies cover the treatment, even if it coverage limit is capped in the policies, overall bill can be split and claimed from 2 or more policies. Speak to customer hotline for the same.

• Does SureClaim pick claim documents from office?
->Yes, employee can give appointment to SureClaim claim advisor to collect documents either from office or from their residence (available only in Bangalore). There are no additional charges for this service.
